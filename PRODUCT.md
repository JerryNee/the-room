# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors to Jianwei's personal site: recruiters, collaborators, friends, and people curious about his mixed-reality, AI, and personal creative work. They arrive expecting a portfolio, but the experience should feel like entering a personal room rather than reading a standard resume page.

## Product Purpose

This site presents Jianwei through an interactive 3D room. The first experience is a standalone door in a white void labeled "Jerry's Room"; entering reveals a desk-like spatial portfolio where objects lead to core sections such as the OS, Gallery, Game Log, and Journey Map.

## Brand Personality

Personal, spatial, curious. The site should feel crafted and playful without becoming gimmicky, and should make Jianwei's technical interests visible through interaction rather than explanatory marketing copy.

## Anti-references

Avoid a Henry clone, a generic retro-game desktop, a normal card-grid portfolio, or a cluttered AI-generated 3D room full of mismatched props. Avoid changes that make the scene feel random, toy-like, or visually careless.

## Design Principles

- Spatial first: major sections should feel like places or objects, not disconnected routes.
- Enter, then explore: the door transition should preserve the feeling of moving between spaces.
- Tactile over decorative: objects should have believable scale, material, and purpose.
- Keep the white void: the world can be surreal and minimal, with the desk scene carrying personality.
- Iterate visibly: 3D changes must be checked from multiple camera angles before they are considered done.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Respect reduced-motion preferences for camera and door animations. Maintain keyboard entry for the door and Escape/back behavior for focused sections. Visible text overlays should remain readable on desktop and mobile.
